Am I a good candidate for rhinoplasty and which approach (open or closed) is best for me?
sure, you would be a good rhinoplasty candidate
Sep 29, 2020
You may be a good candidate for either rhinoplasty or even an injectible rhinopasty. The best way to determine which would be best is a 3D computer simulation. Make sure you find a surgeon with expertise in both surgical and non-surgical options. As for the best approach, let your surgeon decide. Closed approach may work if there are no real changes to the tip needed.
Any suggestions are welcomed with for my lower blepharoplasty?
transconjunctival blepharoplasty
Sep 14, 2020
As long as your eyes are healthy, you should be an excellent candidate for a transconjunctival blepharoplasty with fat repositioning. This can be done under sedation in the office operating room. There is no external scar, and a week or so of bruising. Your surgeon would need to see you after the botox has worn off if possible. Good luck!

What is the best xanthelasma treatment currently available?
surgical excision
Aug 17, 2020
Surgery is the best solution for xanthelasma. This can usually be done under local anesthesia in the office. Eyelid skin heals extremely well. Unfortunately, this condition can recur over several years. If it does, excision can be repeated.

What procedures are available to permanently alter uneven lips?
look to the teeth
Jun 20, 2020
EVERYONE’s face has asymmetries, and yours are actually minimal. We do see them more as we get older. Many lip asymmetries are really due to different tooth shape and projections on each side. Do NOT use a permanent filler, some of the long term issues are disastrous and unfix-able. At times a mucosal advancement can fix volume differences, but I would not recommend that in someone as young as you. You have beautiful lips, and honestly, I don’t think you need a thing!

What are the treatment options for my leaning nose?
go talk with a surgeon
Jul 29, 2019
Your tip deviation is mild, and not uncommon. As the nose grows in adolescence, the septum sometimes grows crooked and can push the tip to one side. If you have problems breathing through your nose, see a facial plastic surgeon. If the shape bothers you, you would be a candidate for cosmetic rhinoplasty once your face is fully grown, at age 18 or so. Good luck!
