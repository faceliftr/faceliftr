Sun bathed three months after rhinoplasty, will this cause permanent swelling?
Swelling after rhinoplasty
Jul 15, 2018
Hello,For the first few months after rhinoplasty any activity that causes swelling of the nose will be  more pronounced than before surgery. This can include sun exposure.  Depending on time of day an hour or two of sun exposure can be significant. It is unlikely that you caused any long-term problems with your nose. Check in with your surgeon. Good luck.JO

Stitches found in my nose 2 years post-op, what do I do?
Sutures after rhinoplasty
Apr 2, 2017
Hello,I would assume that the suture you discovered is a permanent type of suture. Surgeons use these commonly in rhinoplasty. Rarely, one of the sutures will work its way to the surface much like a splinter coming out of your finger. Assuming that you have no other problems such as tenderness or redness you should not have any problems because of this. If you have any questions definitely return to see your surgeon. Good luck.
Is it a good idea to do another rhinoplasty within a year of my last one?
Revision rhinoplasty
Mar 30, 2017
The usual recommendation to wait at least 1 year before revision rhinoplasty is based on the idea that changes will occur in the shape of the tip over that time. So I agree that waiting a year to address issues regarding the nasal tip is reasonable. However, if there is an obvious problem with the nasal dorsum I think a revision can be done sooner because it is unlikely that things will improve to the point where you or your surgeon will be satisfied by waiting.Good luck.

Closed or Open Technique Rhinoplasty
Open Rhinoplasty
Mar 27, 2017
Hello,In my opinion open rhinoplasty offers multiple advantages over closed techniques with no significant long term downside. As a surgeon the ability to see the structures of the nose directly allows more precise manipulation of the cartilage and bone and I feel this translates into a more consistent surgical result. The external scar on the columella heals extremely well and is essentially never a cosmetic issue.Good luck.
