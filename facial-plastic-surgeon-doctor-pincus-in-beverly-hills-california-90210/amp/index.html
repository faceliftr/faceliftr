Can nostril size be reduced without reducing the nasal base width?
Reducing nostril size, but not nasal base
Sep 27, 2020
Yes, reducing the projection of the nose tends to produce two things. The first it produces some flare, which can be reduced without affecting the nasal base width. And the second is that it tends to cause some skin shrinkage along the alar wall (if done correctly).
Can open roof deformity cause breathing problems / post nasal drip? And why do I feel so congeste
Can an open roof deformity cause breathing problems?
Sep 24, 2020
No, an open roof deformity just means that the bony roof of the nose hasn't been closed after a bump has been amputated. Congestion, especially at night, is usually due to turbinate enlargement. It may be on an allergic, irritative or positional basis.

Revision rhinoplasty?
Sep 21, 2020
It his difficult to answer that question based upon one photo and no other history. Were your nostrils asymmetric before, do you have any other cosmetic or functional issues? If your main concern is a droopy tip (which isn't that apparent) and nostril asymmetry, it certainly can be addressed relatively easily with a revision!
What will happen if I fix my mentolabial sulcus?
Sublabial sulcus
Sep 21, 2020
Good question! Firstly, it is rather difficult, though not impossible, to soften that sulcus or groove by filling it. That groove represents the internal attachment of your lower lip. I have tried various fillers, as well as synthetics, to bring it outward with only minimal longterm success. Additionally, you have to be careful when placing a chin implant so that you don't get a "witch's chin" deformity!
Where are spreader grafts harvested from during Rhinoplasty?
Spreader grafts
Sep 19, 2020
Spreader grafts are usually harvested from the nasal septum or ear. They can also be taken from the person's own rib or from a cadaveric rib. It doesn't matter if the septum is deviated or not since the piece taken is usually 1-2mm x 1-2mm x 8-12mm. If the septum were deviated, it could be corrected at the same time, as well.
How long to wait in between surgeries to be safe?
Time between nasal & breast surgery
Sep 18, 2020
I would do the breast surgery first and then the nasal surgery. I do not think there is any problem doing the second surgery as soon as you are up to it.

Does nose tip refining via suture technique have less swelling time the the average 1 year?
Tip refinement
Sep 12, 2020
Generally speaking, it's the access dissection, surgical trauma, time in surgery and post-op care that determine the amount of swelling. Surgical tip refinement is easily performed in a closed fashion. And the vast majority of patients have very little swelling after just a few months!
How long will I have to wait to get silicone and cartilage tip removal?
Nasal silicone explantation
Sep 11, 2020
Generally speaking, the implant can probably be removed any time. I'm assuming that the "tip" you are referring to is part of the implant. However, if you want to have your nose reconstructed at the same time, you would want to wait until all the swelling from that implantation surgery has resolved. This is generally any time after 6 months.

It is hard to say if the smile will return to its pre-implant state because of possible disruption of the muscular attachments. Is my already thin nose a candidate for rhinoplasty?
Rhinoplasty
Sep 9, 2020
Yes, I think you will need de-projection of the tip with slight dorsal reduction. In addition, alar battens to prevent internal valve collapse on inspiration and reversal of the alar retractions using either rim grafts, lateral crural strut grafts and possibly V to Y intranasal vestibular skin advancement.

Yes, the diced cartilage/temporalis fascia graft can be reduced. The problem may be effectively de-projecting the tip. You should discuss the likelihood of success with the surgeon before embarking on the revision.

4th surgery?
Sep 8, 2020
