What went wrong with my tummy tuck?
Before photos would be helpful
Feb 13, 2019
Thank you for the question.  Without the before photos it will be hard to know.  It may be that your skin doesn’t have enough skin elasticity or that not enough skin was removed.  The presence of stretch marks is an indication that the skin does not have normal skin elasticity.
What should I do about internal sutures coming out of my tummy tuck scar?
It depends on what type of sutures they are
Feb 13, 2019
Thank you for the question.  If they are permanent sutures the “spitting“ suture should be removed.  If it’s a dissolvable suture like Monocryl it can be left of place.  Sometime lancing the area with a needle can let the area drain and help heal the area faster.

Healing after TT
Feb 13, 2019
Thank you for the question and photo.  It does not look like the classic signs of an infection.  It may be a hair follicle issue or perhaps a suture knot issue.  You’ll want to have your plastic surgeon take a look to make sure there is nothing that should be done in the interim.
Is the recovery easier with local and twilight sedation?
Recovery should be comparable
Feb 13, 2019
Great question.  The recovery from a TT with twilight should be comparable to general anesthesia.  While twilight may reduce the chance of nausea, the discomfort and fatigue associated with a TT procedure is related to the physical recovery process and not the medications used for anesthesia.

Am I a good candidate for a BBL and other surgeries?
All depends on how much intra-abdominal fat you have
Nov 6, 2018
Great question.  Fat under the skin can be liposuctioned, skin can be removed, and muscles can be tightened.  Fat inside your abdomen called intra-abdominal fat or visceral fat cannot be changed by surgery and requires a chance in diet primarily.  Overall, I think liposuction, tummy tuck, and buttock fat transfer would be the ideal procedures for you (not considering the intra-abdominal fat part).Good luck with your surgery.Dr. Remus Repta
