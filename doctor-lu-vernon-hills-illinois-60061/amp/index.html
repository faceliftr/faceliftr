Is risk of complications too great for someone with an autoimmune disease to get implants?
Breast Implants
Aug 18, 2020
Most of the evidence does not point towards increased complications or exacerbation of autoimmune symptoms in patient’s who get breast implants. Rejection is an immunologic phenomena, and not thought to happen with breast implants. You should have a frank discussion with your plastic surgeon of these issues, as they differ between patients.

Does a person with tight skin have more chance of getting symmastia after breast augmentation?
Breast Augmentation
Aug 12, 2020
Tight skin does not predispose to Symmastia. Factors such as going above the muscle, choosing an implant outside the boundaries of a patients breast base diameter, or over dissection of the implant pocket to try to create more cleavage are predisposing factors. It goes without saying that Symmastia is best avoided, as fixing it is challenging and often requires creating a new pocket for the implant.
I currently have Chronic Myeloid Leukemia. Can I still get plastic surgery?
Breast Augmentation
Aug 12, 2020
Plastic Surgery is elective surgery and patient safety is paramount. Having a chronic disease is not an automatic exclusion for surgery, but I would recommend a discussion with your oncologist regarding the safety of considering elective surgery. If you have a plastic surgeon in mind, he or she can also speak to the oncologist to discuss what the surgery involves, as most oncologists may not understand the details of the surgery.
Prominent veins around areola. Would these get in the way if I want a breast augmentation?
Breast Augmentation
Aug 5, 2020
The vein should not have any effect on a breast augmentation. Visible veins are very common in various areas of the body, including the breast. In some instances if you want a prominent vein removed, you could consider a Phlebectomy, but it is normal to have veins in that area.
Should I wait to finish having kids before I get a breast augmentation or is it okay to go ahead and hav
Breast Augmentation Between Pregnancies
Aug 5, 2020
There is no clear cut answer to whether you should wait to finish having kids before a breast augmentation. Many women have augmentation prior to having children and are still able to breast feed and maintain their breast shape post-pregnancy. However, pregnancy and breast feeding can have unpredictable effects on your breast in terms of size and shape. Therefore, if you are finished having children and then get the breast augmentation, there is one less variable to be concerned about.
