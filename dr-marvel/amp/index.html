Why do some doctors only offer FUE? Isn’t FUT better?
FUE vs. FUT
Feb 8, 2019
Most experienced transplant surgeons offer both surgeries. There are specific indications of why one may be superior to the other. It is true that FUE thins out the donor area, but this can also be the case with repeated strip harvesting procedures. I think if you need a significant amount of hair transplanted then the FUT is the best way to go. Only a consultation can determine the best procedure for you. Thanks for your inquiry.

What happens if I wait too long to get my broken nose fixed & and how bad does hurt to get it fixed?
Timing to correct a broken nose
Apr 2, 2018
It is best to repair a broken nose in the first 2-3 weeks. After a couple of months it doesn't really matter how long you wait. The nose has already healed and will have to be re-broken either way. So waiting longer at this time will be no big deal. If we can get to them at the 2 week period it is perfect. Often times the nose will"snap" back into position without bleeding or excessive bruising or swelling. That's the best time for future reference.

Do I have a collapsed valve? I had rhinoplasty 2 years ago.
Nasal valve collapse
Oct 12, 2017
Based on photos alone I cannot tell if you have nasal valve collapse. It would require an examination. When you inspire and the side wall of the nostril collapses you may in fact have nasal valve collapse. It can easily be determined on a quick physical exam. There is a quick surgical remedy. An implant (Latera) can be placed through a small needle incision in an office setting, and support the collapse thereby correcting the problem. Thanks for your question.

Will medicade, amerigroup cover rhinoplasty?
No
Aug 3, 2017
Amerigroup only covers septoplasty surgery. Any alteration to the outside of the nose is not covered by Medicaid. If you need the inside corrected sometimes your surgeon can correct that part and have it covered by Medicaid (Amerigroup). If you want external work done then you would have to pay out of pocket as that is considered cosmetic.

Is a second plastysmaplasty needed needed during a facelift?
It depends
Aug 3, 2017
If you do not have bands in your neck at rest (that is not flexing your neck muscles), then a second platysmaplasty surgery may not be required. Often times a facelift alone will tighten the skin of the neck without requiring a platysmaplasty. Only a consultation can determine what would be required. If you had it done several years ago (properly) it will probably not be necessary to repeat. Thanks for the question.
