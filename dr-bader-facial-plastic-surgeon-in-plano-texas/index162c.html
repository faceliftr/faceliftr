Do I need a second revision rhinoplasty for tip that keeps drooping?
Revision rhinoplasty
Sep 22, 2020
Maintaining tip projection is a challenging problem, especially in revision cases. It is very unlikely that the tip will stay in the desired position without structural grafting. Septal cartilage is preferred but rib cartilage might be necessary if there is not enough quality cartilage remaining in your septum. Ear cartilage is not appropriate for structural grafting in revision rhinoplasty. I recommend consulting with a very experienced revision rhinoplasty surgeon to see what is possible.
I want this nose so bad. How achieve is it, considering how big my natural nose is?
Is this nose possible?
Aug 26, 2020
It’s possible but not sure it’s desirable. An experienced rhinoplasty surgeon will be able to examine your nose and tell you what is possible to achieve while maintaining nasal function. You don’t want to end up with a nose that looks the way you want but you can’t breathe through. A cute nose that is useless functionally is known as a “hood ornament”. Best of luck.
Which is stronger for tip support in rhinoplasty for asian patients: columella strut graft vs sep
Septal extension graft vs columellar strut
Aug 26, 2020
A columellar strut graft will not provide enough support to maintain projection of an Asian nose in a revision rhinoplasty. Experienced revision rhinoplasty surgeons will recommend changing the shape/dimensions of the septal extension graft (rather than removing it) to create the change you are wanting. Best of luck.
Does harvesting septal cartilage for a columellar strut graft qualify the procedure as a septorhi
Septorhinoplasty
Aug 26, 2020
Harvesting septal cartilage for grafting purposes during a rhinoplasty is very common. Many surgeons will use the terms “rhinoplasty” and “septorhinoplasty” interchangeably. The distinction becomes important if the septum does requiring straightening and an insurance claim is filed. In that scenario, the procedure might be called “septoplasty and rhinoplasty”. The terminology is not as important as making sure you and your surgeon are on the same page regarding the goals of the operation.

Is there a way to create a “chiseled” nasal tip with rhinoplasty?
Chiseled tip rhinoplasty
Nov 7, 2019
The “chiseled” look is a function of the thickness of the skin and soft tissue overlying the cartilage. Patients with thinner skin will always look more “chiseled” compared to patients with thicker skin. There are ways to make the skin a little bit thinner by using retinoic acid topical treatments and reducing soft tissue thickness surgically, but there is a limit to what can be accomplished. An in-person exam would be necessary to determine if your desired look is possible for your nose.

What are the SMAS tightening techniques used with short scar facelifts?
Short scar facelift
Sep 13, 2019
Manipulation of the SMAS is how the lift is achieved in facelift surgery. Any of the SMAS manipulation techniques (plication, imbrication, SMAS-ectomy, high-SMAS, deep plane, etc.) are possible via a short scar technique. The effectiveness and longevity of a facelift can vary between techniques, surgeons and patients. You should consult with a very experienced facelift surgeon who can explain to you why his/her particular SMAS manipulation technique is the best for your individual case.
