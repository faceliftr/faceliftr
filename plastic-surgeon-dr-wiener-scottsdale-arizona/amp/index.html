Seeking a natural facelift - age 53, male. What is best for vertical vs. lateral results?
Natural Facelift
Aug 27, 2020
This is an excellent question. Facelifts in men usually have a more vertical lift rather than a lateral lift. This is done to prevent distortion of your sideburns. A lateral lift can place your hair bearing skin to close to your ear, a telltale sign of a poorly planned facelift. The vertical lift will improve your jowling, nasolabial folds, and lower face skin laxity.
Is a drainless facelift more dangerous?
Drainless Facelift
Aug 27, 2020
Facelifts with or without a drain depends on the Plastic Surgeons preference. I personally place drains on all my facelifts, and remove the drains within 24 hours. I believe that the drains help remove fluid and blood in the first 24 hours, and can contribute to a faster recovery.

Why would a Surgeon choose a SMAS-imbrication over a SMAS-ectomy, where is the logic in keeping ex
SMAS imbrication vs, SMASectomy
Aug 18, 2020
This is a technical issue, that has divided plastic surgeons for many years. Some surgeons prefer imbrication of the SMAS is a safer operation, and has long term excellent results. Other plastic surgeons prefer elevating and excision of a strip of SMAS and then suturing the SMAS back together. This also has long term excellent results. The surgeons skill and experience will help determine the typw of facelift that is best for you.

Will fillers work as well as fat transfer during facelift?
Fillers vs. Fat Transfer during facelift
Jul 20, 2020
This is an excellent question that is often asked. I prefer fat transfer at the time of surgery, rather than adding fillers during surgery. With a BMI of 17 you are very thin, and it can be a challenge to harvest and transfer fat. Fat transfer is permanent once the fat becomes vascularized, and not all fat survives the transfer. Fillers, and there are many options, can always be added after all the swelling from the facelift resolves.
